Cosmos World Foundation Models

A family of pre-trained world foundation models (WFMs) purpose-built for generating physics-aware videos and world states for Physical AI. Cosmos 1.0 ships two parallel tracks — diffusion and autoregressive transformers — both operating in the latent space of the Cosmos Tokenizer, with Text2World and Video2World variants at 4B–14B scale.

02 — Model family

Eight checkpoints across two paradigms

Cosmos 1.0 exposes two scalable deep-learning paradigms in parallel. Each paradigm has a base model (text-conditioned future-frame generation) and a derivative Video2World model that adds first-frame conditioning. A prompt upsampler accompanies the diffusion track; a diffusion decoder accompanies the autoregressive track.

03 — End-to-end pipeline

From text or video in, to a 5-second world out

Both tracks share a common scaffold: a video encoder produces a compact latent, a transformer stack denoises (diffusion) or unrolls (autoregressive) over that latent, and a paired decoder reconstructs frames. Text grounding flows in through cross-attention from a frozen T5-XXL encoder. Guardrails wrap the pipeline on both ends.

Figure 1. Common Cosmos 1.0 pipeline. Diffusion uses the continuous tokenizer (CV8×8×8); autoregressive uses the discrete one (DV8×16×16). Block count, hidden size, head count and FFN width are not given in the HF blog post or model card; see §12 caveats.
04 — Cosmos Tokenizer

One tokenizer suite, two compression regimes

The Cosmos Tokenizer is the substrate on which both tracks operate. Two variants are released for video, sized for the two paradigms: a continuous tokenizer for diffusion latents and a discrete tokenizer for autoregressive token streams. Both shrink video on a fixed compression grid.

Figure 2. Compression rates per HF blog. Codebook size, latent channel count, and tokenizer parameter counts: unknown in source inventory.

Why two tokenizers

Continuous latents are well-suited to diffusion's Gaussian-noise denoising objective; discrete codes are well-suited to next-token cross-entropy. Cosmos picks the higher spatial compression (16×16) for the discrete tokenizer so the resulting token sequence stays tractable for an autoregressive transformer.

Reported behaviour

  • CV8×8×8121 frames/pass · diffusion
  • DV8×16×1649 frames/pass · autoregressive
  • CurationNeMo Curator pipeline · 20M hrs in 14 days on Blackwell
Source: HF blog §"High-Fidelity Compression with Cosmos Tokenizer".
05 — Diffusion architecture

Latent diffusion transformer with AdaLN-LoRA conditioning

Cosmos-1.0-Diffusion is a diffusion transformer that denoises in the latent space of Cosmos-1.0-Tokenizer-CV8x8x8. Each block is a stack of self-attention, cross-attention, and feed-forward MLP, modulated by adaptive layer normalization (AdaLN) keyed on the diffusion time step t.

Figure 3. Diffusion transformer block (reconstructed from HF blog Figure 2 description). AdaLN-LoRA predicts the (scale, shift, gate) modulation triples for all three sub-blocks from a low-rank projection of the timestep embedding.

Reported tricks

  • 3D Patchifynon-overlapping 3D patches → token sequence; preserves spatial & temporal relations
  • FPS-aware 3D RoPEpositional encoding across (T,H,W); adapts to varying frame rates
  • Q-K NormRMSNorm on Q and K to prevent attention collapse
  • AdaLN-LoRAlow-rank modulation cuts 36% of params (11B → 7B)
  • Cross-attnT5-XXL embeddings fed to every block
  • Time cond.diffusion timestep t conditions AdaLN scale/shift/gate

Sampling, in equation form

The forward (noising) and reverse (denoising) processes are not detailed numerically in the model card or HF blog. The schedule, predictor parameterization (ε-pred vs v-pred vs x₀-pred), and number of sampling steps are not reported in source inventory. Conceptually:

z_t = α(t)·z₀ + σ(t)·ε, ε ~ N(0, I) // forward z_{t-1} ← Dθ( z_t, t, T5(prompt), [z_cond] ) // reverse, transformer Dθ x̂ = TokenizerDec( z₀ ) // CV8×8×8 decoder

06 — Autoregressive architecture

Discrete next-token video transformer

The autoregressive track encodes video into discrete tokens via Cosmos-1.0-Tokenizer-DV8x16x16, embeds them, and processes them through repeated transformer blocks with absolute positional embeddings, 3D RoPE, optional cross-attention, and a 2-layer MLP. The tokenizer decoder reconstructs the predicted video.

Figure 4. Autoregressive transformer block, reconstructed from HF blog Figure 1: "discrete tokens transformed into learned embeddings, processed through repeated transformer blocks, each consisting of absolute positional embedding and 3D RoPE components that are flattened before entering the self-attention module. Each block also includes a cross-attention module that incorporates encoded text prompts (via T5), followed by a two-layer MLP."
Source: HF blog §"Autoregressive Models" · §"Real-time Inference Performance".
07 — Attention & sequence handling

3D RoPE, FPS-aware on the diffusion side; YaRN-extended on the AR side

Cosmos uses standard self- and cross-attention but customizes the positional encoding for video: each track packs a (time, height, width) coordinate triple per token and applies a 3D variant of rotary position embedding (RoPE).

Diffusion side

FPS-aware 3D RoPE on patch tokens

After 3D patchification, every patch gets a (t, h, w) coordinate. RoPE is applied along all three axes. The variant is FPS-aware: the temporal axis is scaled by frame rate, so the same physical second occupies the same phase regardless of input fps. This enables progressive training across 512p → 720p and multi-aspect-ratio buckets.

  • Self-attnover flattened 3D patch sequence
  • Cross-attnQ from latent, K/V from T5-XXL
  • Q-K NormRMSNorm on Q, K (attention collapse)
  • Mask / sparsityunknown in source inventory

Autoregressive side

Absolute PE + 3D RoPE, then YaRN extension

Discrete tokens get an absolute positional embedding and a 3D RoPE component; both are flattened before attention. Stage 1 uses a 17-frame context (1 input → 16 predicted). Stage 1.1 doubles the context to 34 frames by applying the YaRN rotary extension to the temporal axis — letting the model see longer dependencies without retraining from scratch.

  • Self-attncausal over discrete token stream
  • Cross-attnonly on Video2World variants (Stage 2)
  • KV cachestandard AR behavior; details not reported
  • GQA / MQAunknown in source inventory
Source: HF blog §"Diffusion Models", §"Autoregressive Models", §"Pre-training of Cosmos Autoregressive WFMs".
08 — Training stages

Multi-stage pretraining; cooling down on high-quality pairs

Autoregressive pretraining

STAGE 1
Video prediction

Predict 16 future frames given 1 input frame. Context length = 17 frames. Resolution 640 × 1024.

STAGE 1.1
Long-context extension

Context length grows to 34 frames via YaRN extension on temporal RoPE — longer video dependencies, no retrain.

STAGE 2
Text conditioning

Add cross-attention layers. Joint image + video training with 34-frame context. Larger batches for images.

COOLDOWN
High-quality refinement

Linear LR decay to zero over 30 000 iterations on curated high-quality image–video pairs.

Diffusion pretraining

A
Joint image + video

Domain-specific latent normalization aligns image and video latent distributions for joint training.

B
Progressive resolution

Start at 512p, transition to 720p with increased frame counts. Then fine-tune on high-quality subsets.

C
Multi-aspect buckets

Aspect-ratio buckets (e.g. 1:1, 16:9). Reflection padding preserves content during resize.

D
Mixed precision

BF16 weights for speed, FP32 stability path to minimize loss spikes.

E
Conditioning

T5-XXL for Text2World. Prior frames concatenated with augment noise for image / video conditioning.

Pretraining data

Scale

20M hours of real-world human-interaction, environment, industrial, robotics and driving video — equivalent to ≈ 9,000 trillion tokens.

Compute

10,000 NVIDIA H100 GPUs over ≈ 3 months. Data curation via NeMo Curator: 20M hrs processed in 14 days on Blackwell.

Coverage

Hand motions, object manipulation, spatial awareness, navigation, camera movements. Curation optimized for Physical AI downstream tasks.

Post-training

The HF blog describes two-stage downstream use rather than alignment-style post-training of the base model:

  • GeneralistBase Cosmos WFMs trained on diverse data — broad coverage of physics and natural dynamics.
  • SpecialistDeveloper-side fine-tunes for AV, humanoid robotics, night scenes with emergency vehicles, industrial robotics, etc. Uses NVIDIA NeMo Framework.
  • RL / SFT / distillation on basenot reported in source inventory
