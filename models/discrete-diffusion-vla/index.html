Discrete Diffusion VLA Architecture

A single-transformer policy that models discretized action chunks with discrete diffusion and is trained with the same cross-entropy objective as its VLM backbone. Action generation runs inside the unified transformer rather than through a bolted-on diffusion/flow head, while preserving diffusion's progressive refinement via iterative re-masking.

§02

Action discretization & chunk anatomy

Each continuous control dimension is binned, then H future timesteps are packed into a fixed-length token chunk. The vocabulary is extended with a single mask symbol M for the diffusion forward process.

OpenVLA-style binning, extended for masked-diffusion

Per-dimension quantile bins from the 1st–99th percentile of the training data avoid outlier saturation. A single timestep emits Dact = 7 tokens (xyz translation · rpy rotation · binary gripper). H future timesteps stack into a fixed-length sequence of L = H × Dact positions — so every action chunk has identical length and needs no [EOS] padding.

For the discrete-diffusion forward process, the action vocabulary V = K + 1 adds a special mask symbol M = [MASK] used to corrupt action positions during training and to initialize them at inference.

§04

Secondary re-masking · uncertainty revisited

Beyond the schedule-driven keep ratio, two lightweight checks scan previously committed tokens every round. A failed check sends a position back to [MASK] so the next round can revise it.

CHECK · I

Absolute-confidence threshold ηtabs

If a previously committed token's current max-confidence falls below a monotonically increasing step-dependent threshold ηtabs, the token is re-masked. Later rounds demand higher confidence before a commit can stand.

CHECK · II

Residual-drop check

If a committed position's confidence drops sharply relative to its previous round, the prediction is unstable across rounds and is re-masked. Together with the threshold check, this enforces cross-step consistency and corrects early errors.

§07

Caveats & unverified specifics

Items below are either not stated by arXiv:2508.20072 or are documented only as "follows OpenVLA / Prismatic-7B" without a concrete value in the version reviewed here.

caveats

Treat as inherited-from-OpenVLA unless the paper states otherwise

verify against current arXiv revision before citing exact numbers
Parameter countunstated
The paper does not give a single "total parameters" number for Discrete Diffusion VLA. The OpenVLA / Prismatic-7B backbone it builds on is a 7B-scale VLM, but the precise count for this work (incl. projector, action head) is not enumerated in the version reviewed.
Context lengthunstated
Total input sequence length depends on chosen image resolution, number of camera views, instruction length, and L = H · 7 action positions. No single max-context figure is reported.
Action chunk horizon Hvaries
The paper denotes a generic chunk horizon H and notes settings per benchmark; the exact H used for LIBERO / Fractal / Bridge should be confirmed from the experiments section of the latest arXiv version.
Action bins Kstated
K = 256 per dimension, quantile bins over the 1st–99th percentile, plus a binary gripper. Vocabulary V = 257 (adds [MASK]). Inherited tokenization scheme from RT-1 / OpenVLA.
Visual encodersinherited
SigLIP and DINOv2 ViTs as in OpenVLA / Prismatic-7B — not novel to this work. Specific ViT sizes follow the Prismatic-7B recipe; treat them as backbone-derived, not contributions of the discrete-diffusion method.
Robot embodimentbenchmark-bound
Evaluations cover Franka Panda (LIBERO), Google Robot (SimplerEnv-Fractal), and WidowX (SimplerEnv-Bridge). Generalization beyond these embodiments is not demonstrated in the reviewed version.
