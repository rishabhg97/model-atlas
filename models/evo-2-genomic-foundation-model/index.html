Genomic foundation model · 2025-02-19

Evo 2: a DNA language model that reads, writes, and reasons across 1,000,000 nucleotides at single-base resolution.

A StripedHyena 2 autoregressive sequence model pretrained on OpenGenome2 — the largest open biological corpus to date. Built by Arc Institute & NVIDIA, with collaborators at Stanford, UC Berkeley, and UCSF. Code, data, and weights released under Apache-2.0.

03 / End-to-end model map

From a raw genome to a base-resolution likelihood

Evo 2 is a single causal sequence model — no separate encoder or task head architecture. Every downstream behaviour (variant scoring, embeddings, generation, design) is the same forward pass, sampled or read out differently.

04 / Backbone

StripedHyena 2 — a convolutional multi-hybrid

StripedHyena 2 alternates input-dependent Hyena convolutions with periodic attention layers. The 2025 ML paper accompanying Evo 2 frames it as a "convolutional multi-hybrid language model at scale." Exact block-level numerics are not published in the listed sources.

One alternating stripe (schematic)

Layer schedule is reported as alternating Hyena and attention; the precise periodicity, filter parameterisation, and head counts are not enumerated in the announcement or repo README.

What the stripe pattern buys you

  • Hyena layers replace quadratic attention with an input-dependent long convolution. Cost scales O(L log L) instead of O(L²), which is what makes a 1 M-token context tractable.
  • Mixing multiple Hyena bandwidths (long / medium / short, hence "multi-hybrid") lets a single layer capture both local motifs and distant regulatory dependencies.
  • Periodic attention stripes restore exact local mixing where it matters — e.g. tight base-pair interactions and coding-frame patterns.
  • The block schedule was co-designed with NVIDIA to keep FP8 utilisation high on H100 — the announcement reports training across > 2,000 H100 GPUs on DGX Cloud via AWS.

05 / Long-context

One million nucleotides in a single forward pass

Three checkpoints — evo2_40b, evo2_20b, evo2_7b — accept the full 1 M-nt context. The shorter "_base" checkpoints train at 8K and the _262k variant at 256K, illustrating the staged context-extension curriculum.

Why it matters: 1 M nt is comparable in scale to a small bacterial genome. Evo 2 can therefore reason about whole-operon, whole-locus, and even whole-genome dependencies in one shot — what the announcement summarises as "relationships between distant parts of a genome."

06 / Training & data

Pretrained autoregressively on OpenGenome2

No RLHF. No instruction tuning. Evo 2 is a single-objective causal language model over nucleotides. The fine-tuned evo2_7b_microviridae checkpoint is the only post-trained variant the sources list.

Pretraining

Stage 01
Causal next-nucleotide prediction over OpenGenome2. Trained on the NVIDIA DGX Cloud AI platform via AWS, using > 2,000 NVIDIA H100 GPUs, for "several months." Framework: Savanna.

Context-length extension

Stage 02
Released as separate checkpoints: 8K → 256K → 1M (8× the longest Evo 1 context). Curriculum specifics not enumerated in listed sources.

Domain fine-tuning

Stage 03 · optional
evo2_7b_microviridae is the only published fine-tune in the released checkpoint set — a 7 B base further trained on Microviridae phage genomes.

Post-training (RLHF, SFT, distillation)

Stage 04
Not reported in listed sources — none of these stages are mentioned in the announcement or README.
07 / Capabilities

Three usage patterns from one forward pass

The README exposes exactly three call surfaces — forward, return_embeddings=True, and generate. Every downstream task in the announcement decomposes into one of these.

01 · Likelihood scoring

Variant effect prediction

Score the per-position likelihood under the reference vs. alternative allele. Used in the published BRCA1 zero-shot notebook to call pathogenic vs benign mutations without any task-specific training.

outputs, _ = evo2_model(input_ids)
logits = outputs[0]
# shape: (B, L, V)

02 · Embeddings

Functional / structural features

Return intermediate-layer activations (e.g. blocks.28.mlp.l3) as fixed-dim representations for any downstream classifier — exon prediction is the worked example. The paper notes intermediate embeddings outperform the final layer.

outputs, embeddings = evo2_model(
  input_ids,
  return_embeddings=True,
  layer_names=['blocks.28.mlp.l3'])

03 · Generation

Genome design

Autoregressive sampling with optional phylogenetic species tags as prompts. Per the announcement, Evo 2 "is capable of designing new genomes that are as long as the genomes of simple bacteria."

evo2_model.generate(
  prompt_seqs=["ACGT"],
  n_tokens=400,
  temperature=1.0,
  top_k=4)

09 / Mechanistic interpretability

SAE-based feature visualiser, in collaboration with Goodfire

Arc Institute shipped an interpretability tool alongside the model — a sparse-autoencoder probe of Evo 2's intermediate activations — and a public visualiser. The Goodfire blog post on the project is referenced from the announcement.

Tooling

Mechanistic interpretability visualiser

Hosted at arcinstitute.org/tools/evo/evo-mech-interp. Surfaces the SAE-discovered biological features the model uses to recognise genomic patterns.

  • Designer companion product: Evo Designer — user-friendly interface to interact with the model.
  • NIM/BioNeMo hosted API: build.nvidia.com/arc/evo2-40b for 40B inference without local install.

SAE notebook

What the notebook demonstrates

The notebooks/sparse_autoencoder example walks through running the released SAE on Evo 2 activations and visualising features that fire on a region of the E. coli genome. Read at github.com/ArcInstitute/evo2.

Feature count, hidden-dimension expansion ratio, and exact training data of the SAE are not reported in the announcement or README.

10 / Unknowns & caveats

What the listed sources do not say

Two of the three listed sources are user-facing pages; the third is a backlog row. Where they disagree we preserve both; where neither speaks we mark "not reported."

Source disagreement: pretraining token count
The Arc Institute announcement states Evo 2 is "trained on over 9.3 trillion tokens – in this case, nucleotides – from over 128 thousand genomes." The ArcInstitute/evo2 README states OpenGenome2 contains "8.8 trillion tokens from all domains of life." Both numbers are reproduced verbatim on this page. The atlas does not pick one over the other.
Tokenizer alphabet & vocab sizeThe README shows the tokenizer API but does not enumerate the vocabulary. Whether N, ambiguity codes, or species/phylogenetic tags are dedicated tokens is not stated in the listed sources.
Per-block dimensionsNumber of StripedHyena 2 blocks, hidden dimension, head count, Hyena filter parameterisation, and MLP expansion ratio are not enumerated in the announcement or README for any checkpoint.
Stripe scheduleThe README confirms the architecture name (StripedHyena 2) and the qualitative pattern of alternating Hyena and attention layers; the exact period and ordering is left to the linked StripedHyena 2 paper.
Training compute & duration"Several months" on "over 2,000 H100 GPUs" via DGX Cloud / AWS. Exact GPU-hours, TFLOP totals, optimizer, batch size, learning-rate schedule: not reported in listed sources.
Per-domain corpus compositionOpenGenome2 is reported as covering bacteria, archaea, phage, eukarya, and metagenomic data; the numerical split by domain is not published in the listed sources.
Benchmark suite beyond BRCA1The announcement reports the BRCA1 > 90% accuracy headline only. Detailed per-task scores live in the paper, which is referenced but not in the listed-sources set for this atlas.
