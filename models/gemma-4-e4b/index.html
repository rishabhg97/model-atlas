Gemma 4 E4B Edge

Gemma 4 E4B is the larger of the two edge-scale dense decoders in the Gemma 4 family, released April 2, 2026 alongside the 26B-A4B MoE and 31B Dense flagships under Apache 2.0. The local inventory lead is terse: “Dense edge-scale Transformer; GQA; local-global attention.” — size not explicit beyond the “E4B” name, layer / head / dim counts unknown, and no primary-source model card was confirmed at build time. This page therefore separates two kinds of fact: (a) shared-family mechanisms that the Gemma 4 model card describes for every variant in the family (local-global interleave, p-RoPE + unified KV on global layers, GQA, QK-Norm, configurable thinking mode, audio & vision encoders on the edge variants), and (b) per-checkpoint shapes specific to E4B — all marked not reported or unknown in source inventory until a Google DeepMind primary source confirms them.

02 · End-to-end map

From three input modalities to a text logit vector.

A prompt arrives as an interleaved sequence of text tokens, image regions, and on the E-tier variants only, audio segments. Image and audio inputs are lifted into the language-model token stream by their respective encoders; the joint sequence then passes through the dense decoder stack and exits as logits over the Gemma vocabulary. 04 · Decoder block internals

Pre-norm transformer block with a dense SwiGLU MLP — not MoE.

E4B is the dense edge variant: the feed-forward sub-block is a single SwiGLU MLP at every layer, not a 128-expert MoE like the 26B-A4B. The block structure follows the Gemma 4 family arrangement: pre-norm with RMSNorm, grouped-query attention with QK-Norm, and residual paths around attention and feed-forward. Numerical shapes — d_model, head counts, MLP intermediate width — are not reported in the source inventory and are deliberately not invented below.

1 · Pre-attention RMSNorm
RMSNorm

Pre-norm transformer arrangement consistent with the Gemma 4 family: normalize the residual stream before entering the attention sub-block. Learned scale γ, no bias.

2 · Grouped-query attention
GQA · inventory lead

Multiple query heads share each KV head. Head counts (n_q, n_kv, head_dim) for E4B are not stated in the source inventory; GQA itself is the Raschka-table attribute carried forward.

3 · QK-Norm
per-head RMSNorm *

RMSNorm applied to the per-head queries and keys before the dot product. Inferred from the Gemma 4 family pattern; not separately confirmed for E4B in the source inventory.

4 · Local or global attention
window size: not reported

Layer type is fixed at config time. Local attends only to a sliding window of recent tokens; global attends over the full causal context and additionally applies p-RoPE and unified KV per the family rule.

5 · Residual add
x + Attn(x)

Standard residual connection around the attention sub-block.

6 · Pre-MLP RMSNorm
RMSNorm

Second RMSNorm in the pre-norm sandwich, before the MLP sub-block.

7 · SwiGLU MLP
d_ff not reported

Dense feed-forward: W_down(SiLU(W_gate·h) ⊙ (W_up·h)). Three projections per layer rather than the routed 128-expert bank used in the 26B-A4B sibling. Intermediate width d_ff is not reported in the source inventory.

8 · Residual add
x + MLP(x)

Residual closes the block. Output goes to the next layer or, at the final layer, to RMSNorm + LM head.

05 · Local vs global attention

Two attention regimes alternating along the stack.

The Gemma 4 family text describes its attention as a hybrid mechanism that interleaves local sliding-window attention with full global attention, ensuring the final layer is always global. The two regimes share QKV projection geometry but differ in mask, positional encoding, and KV caching. E4B inherits this family design; the numeric specifics (window size, cadence, total depth) for the E4B checkpoint specifically are not in the source inventory.

Local sliding-window attention

Each query attends only over the most recent W tokens of context. Cheap, constant-memory per token, regardless of how long the full prompt is — central to making an edge-scale model viable for on-device inference.

06 · p-RoPE & unified KV

Two memory tricks the family applies to global layers only.

The Gemma 4 family model card calls out two long-context optimisations applied only to global layers: Proportional RoPE (p-RoPE) for positional encoding, and unified Keys and Values in the KV cache. Local layers do not carry these. For E4B the source inventory does not state the positional-encoding name — the diagram below describes the family rule, marked with an asterisk to flag shared-family inference, and the formal frequency-scaling recipe is labeled not reported.

Long-context memory · global layers only *

Proportional RoPE + shared K / V

p-RoPE. The Gemma 4 family text names Proportional RoPE as the positional encoding used on global layers. The precise frequency-scaling recipe is not stated for E4B in the source inventory; the model-card phrasing pairs p-RoPE explicitly with long-context behaviour, suggesting per-dimension base frequencies scaled with context length.

Unified Keys and Values. Global layers tie the K and V projections so they share storage in the KV cache, roughly halving the per-token cache footprint at those layers. Where the global count is small relative to total depth, this saving compounds where it matters most.

Why only on global layers? Global attention is the bottleneck at long context — its KV cache grows with the full sequence. Local layers cap at the window size, so their cache is already bounded; the optimisations target the layers where memory actually hurts. This matters more, not less, on the edge tier.

Global-layer attention · sketch
K = V = W_KV · h // unified projection *
Q = W_Q · h
Q', K' = p-RoPE(Q, K, pos) // proportional rotation *
A = softmax( Q' · K'ᵀ / √d_h ) · V // causal · full context

08 · Audio encoder

The E-tier’s headline differentiator: audio in.

Of all four Gemma 4 variants, only the two edge models — E2B and E4B — embed an audio encoder. The 26B-A4B and 31B Dense flagships do not. The encoder converts a waveform into audio tokens that are interleaved into the joint sequence the decoder consumes. The encoder backbone, audio sample rate, audio-tokens-per-second, and any preprocessing front-end are not reported in the source inventory.

09 · Thinking mode

Configurable reasoning channel inherited from the family.

Like the rest of the Gemma 4 family, E4B is shipped with a configurable thinking mode: include a trigger token at the start of the system prompt and the model writes its internal reasoning into a separate channel before producing the final answer. Sampling defaults and exact channel-delimiter token strings for E4B specifically are not in the source inventory; the family convention is used as a placeholder, marked with an asterisk.

12 · Caveats & unknowns

The source inventory commits to seven facts. Everything else is open.

This page treats the local inventory as a starting lead, not a primary-source dossier. The caveats below enumerate exactly what was not available at build time. Anything below should be replaced with source-backed text once a Google DeepMind primary source for Gemma 4 E4B is located.

No primary source located

No Google DeepMind model card, technical report, release blog, or HF repository for the literal string “Gemma 4 E4B” was confirmed at build time. The inventory entry is the only commitment to the model's existence at this naming. Confirm the canonical model-card URL and HF repo path before quoting any value here.

Active & total parameter count

The inventory states parameter scale: edge-scale, size not explicit beyond name and active parameter scale: unknown in source inventory. The literal “E4B” name is suggestive of ~4 B parameters but is not source-confirmed; for a dense model the active and total counts coincide regardless.

Layer count, hidden size, head geometry

d_model, n_layers, n_heads, n_kv_heads, head_dim, and d_ff are all unknown in source inventory. The HF config file would be the canonical source. The 5 : 1 local-to-global cadence visualised here is shared-family pattern, not E4B-confirmed positions.

Window size and context length

The sliding-window size for local layers and the maximum context for global layers are both not reported. Family text suggests an edge tier shorter than the flagship's long-context window, but the exact figures for E4B are not stated.

Positional encoding

p-RoPE for global layers is a family rule and is marked here with an asterisk. The positional scheme used on local layers in E4B is not separately described; whether it is p-RoPE, vanilla RoPE, or implicit-via-window is unknown in source inventory.

Vocabulary & tokenizer

The inventory leaves both blank. The Gemma 4 family-shared 262 144 vocab and SentencePiece tokenizer are the working assumption only; do not quote either as an E4B-confirmed value until the HF tokenizer artifacts are inspected.

Vision & audio encoders

Both modality encoders are confirmed to exist on the E-tier by the family text. Their parameter counts, backbones (e.g. SigLIP / a custom audio backbone), sample-rate / patch-size details, and tokens-per-second are not reported for E4B in the source inventory.

Training token budget & recipe

Token count, distillation source (if any), optimiser, schedule, post-training mix, RL stage, and safety-evaluation regime are not stated for E4B. The family blog typically credits the broader Gemini research line without giving numbers; replace this caveat when the model card lands.

Benchmarks

No source-reported benchmark numbers were available at build time. Numbers must be added behind explicit eval-suite labels (suite, version, shot count, tool-use status, judge) and clearly flagged source-reported vs independent.
