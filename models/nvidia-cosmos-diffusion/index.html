NVIDIA Cosmos Diffusion
World generation for Physical AI — Text2World & Video2World, 7B / 14B

Cosmos-1.0-Diffusion is NVIDIA's family of latent diffusion World Foundation Models built for generating physics-aware videos of visual worlds. Two parallel lanes — Text2World from a prompt and Video2World conditioned on a first frame — both denoise in the latent space of the Cosmos Tokenizer (CV8×8×8) and emit 121-frame, 5-second clips at up to 1280×704. Released under the NVIDIA Open Model License for commercial use.

§ 02

Text2World — end-to-end pipeline

Prompt is encoded by T5-XXL, the latent video grid is initialized as pure Gaussian noise, the DiT denoises it across T sampling steps with text injected via cross-attention, and the Cosmos Tokenizer decoder reconstructs pixels.
Text2World Prompt → latent denoise → decoded video pθ(x0 | ε, ctext)
A · PROMPT
Text input (< 300 words)
Scene description, key objects, background, motions over the 5 s clip. Optionally rewritten by the prompt upsampler for denser, more cinematic phrasing.
user prompt
"A sleek humanoid robot stands in a warehouse… glowing blue light from its chest… shallow depth of field…"
B · ENCODE
T5-XXL text embeddings
Tokens are encoded by the frozen T5-XXL encoder into a per-token embedding sequence that all DiT cross-attention layers read from.
c_text · (Ltxt, d)
C · INIT
Latent noise grid xT ~ N(0,I)
A (1+T'/8) × H/8 × W/8 × 16 continuous latent tensor is filled with Gaussian noise. With the CV8×8×8 tokenizer, a 121-frame clip yields 16 latent time-steps.
xT · latent grid
D · DENOISE
DiT loop, T sampling steps
Each step: adaLN embeds time t, self-attention mixes the spatio-temporal latent, cross-attention injects T5 text features, FFN refines. The same network is called every step.
xt→t-1 · partial
E · DECODE
CV8×8×8 decoder → video
The clean latent x0 is decoded by the Cosmos Tokenizer to 121 RGB frames (5 s · 24 fps · 1280×704). A post-hoc guardrail filters the rendered video.
decoded · 121 frames
xT ∈ ℝ16×88×40×16 latent dim = 16 CV8×8×8 (spatial 8× · temporal 8×) Latent grid sizes shown for 1280×704 at the default 121-frame setting; resolution and frame count are configurable.
§ 03

Video2World — end-to-end pipeline

Same DiT, same tokenizer, same T5-XXL — but the latent tensor is built by concatenating the conditioning frame's latents to the noise along the temporal axis, and an "augment noise" step keeps the model from overtrusting the clean condition at inference.
Video2World First frame + prompt → predicted next 120 frames pθ(x0 | zcond, ctext)
A · INPUTS
Image + prompt
User provides one image (the "current observation") plus a short text describing how the world should evolve. Future-only prediction is the V2W contract.
conditioning
+ text
B · TOKENIZE
Encode the conditioning frame
The single image is passed through the Cosmos Tokenizer encoder to produce its latent representation zcond.
zcond
C · BUILD
Concat zcond with noise · add augment noise
Conditional latents are placed at the temporal head; remaining slots are Gaussian noise. Augment noise is added on zcond to match the noisy distribution seen in training.
[zcond ‖ ε ]T
D · DENOISE
DiT loop on the joint latent
Same DiT block stack as Text2World; self-attention now spans both the conditioning and noise frames, so the conditioning leaks through every step and constrains the world.
xt→t-1 · joint
E · DECODE
Decode predicted 120 future frames
The conditioning slot is discarded; the predicted latents are decoded by CV8×8×8 to 120 future RGB frames at the same default resolution and frame rate as T2W.
120 future frames
conditioning via temporal concat augment_noise on zcond cond frames = 1 · predicted = 120 Video2World checkpoints are fine-tunes of the corresponding Text2World base; lane share weights everywhere except the input-handling logic.
§ 04

Cosmos Tokenizer — the latent space both lanes denoise in

Diffusion happens in the latent space of the continuous video tokenizer CV8×8×8: 8× spatial and 8× temporal compression, 16-dim continuous latent, temporally causal so the same model also tokenizes single images (T = 0).

Encoder–decoder with a causal, wavelet front-end

The tokenizer is built as a lightweight encoder–decoder that processes only current and past frames. A 2-level 3D Haar wavelet downsamples the input 4× and groups frames as {x0, x1:4, x5:8, …}; subsequent stages apply spatio-temporally factorized causal 3D convolutions (1×k×k spatial → k×1×1 temporal with left-padding k−1) and a causal spatio-temporal self-attention. The decoder mirrors the encoder.

§ 05

Inside one DiT block

Both lanes share the same building block: interleaved self-attention, cross-attention (text), and feed-forward layers, with adaptive layer norm embedding the diffusion timestep t before each sub-layer.
Diffusion-transformer block (one of N)
Each layer reads the latent video tokens (post-patchify) and the T5-XXL text embeddings; adaLN is conditioned on the diffusion time-step t.

§ 06

Surrounding platform — only what the diffusion stack actually uses

Cosmos is a broader Physical-AI platform (tokenizers, world models, guardrails, data pipeline, transfer/post-training recipes). These four components sit directly on the inference path of the diffusion lanes.
A · Text encoder
T5-XXL
Encodes the user prompt into per-token embeddings. Read by every DiT block via cross-attention. Released checkpoints assume this exact encoder; can be offloaded between runs to free GPU memory.
frozen · offloadable
B · Prompt rewriter
Prompt upsampler
Optional LLM-based rewriter that expands a short user prompt into the dense, descriptive style the model was trained on (training prompts were generated by a VLM at video-description time).
optional · offloadable
C · Latent codec
Cosmos Tokenizer · CV8×8×8
Encoder turns conditioning images/videos into latents; decoder maps the denoised x0 back to pixels. Causal, joint image+video, 16-dim continuous AE. See §04 for the full breakdown.
~105M params · offloadable
D · Safety
Cosmos-1.0-Guardrail
Mandatory pre- and post-filter. Tampering with it (bypass, disable, weaken) terminates the Open Model License grant. Released as a separate HF model card alongside the diffusion checkpoints.
required · not optional

Unknowns, caveats, and license boundaries

Watch-outs

Sources: HF model cards (7B/14B · T2W/V2W) · arXiv:2501.03575 · research.nvidia.com/labs/cosmos-lab
  • DiT internals Exact depth, hidden dim, head count, FFN size, position-embedding scheme, and number of sampling steps are not stated on the HF model cards. Detailed configs live in the technical report and the NVIDIA/Cosmos repo's config files.
  • Sampler & CFG Specific diffusion sampler, classifier-free-guidance scale, and noise schedule used at inference are not pinned on the model cards. Defaults live in the released code.
  • Augment noise The exact augment-noise distribution applied to conditional latents in Video2World is described qualitatively only ("bridge the training–inference gap"). Hyperparameters live in the released code.
  • Tokenizer for diffusion Verified: Cosmos-1.0-Tokenizer-CV8×8×8 is the tokenizer used by the diffusion track. The discrete DV* tokenizers are paired with the separate autoregressive Cosmos track — they are not the path here.
  • Training data The paper describes a broad Cosmos data pipeline (~20M hours of video at the platform level); the model cards do not break out per-checkpoint pre-training tokens or curation. Treat any per-model number as platform-level unless cited.
  • License NVIDIA Open Model License (Jan 6, 2025) — commercial use, derivatives, and redistribution are permitted; NVIDIA claims no ownership of outputs. Attribution "Built on NVIDIA Cosmos" is required for downstream products.
  • Guardrail Cosmos-1.0-Guardrail is a license condition, not an option. Bypassing or weakening it auto-terminates the grant.
  • Bias mitigation The model cards explicitly state "None" for bias-mitigation measures and protected-class participation in design/testing. Downstream users are responsible for fitness-for-purpose review.
  • Platform ≠ checkpoints The paper's "Cosmos WFM Platform" describes both diffusion and autoregressive WFM lanes plus transfer/post-training recipes. Only the four diffusion checkpoints (7B/14B · T2W/V2W) are scoped on this page; AR-track variants are out of scope.
  • Output realism NVIDIA acknowledges the model "may not follow the video input accurately" (V2W) and may produce content considered toxic, offensive, or indecent if unguarded. The guardrail is the contractual mitigation.
