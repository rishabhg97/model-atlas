Qwen2 7B — Architecture brief

Source: arXiv:2407.10671 Qwen Team, Alibaba Jul 2024
Open weights · Decoder-only · Dense

A dense Transformer with GQA, SwiGLU and RoPE.

Qwen2-7B is one of four dense variants released in the Qwen2 family (0.5B, 1.5B, 7B, 72B), alongside a 57B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts sibling. All five share the same primitive: a Llama-style decoder-only Transformer with Grouped-Query Attention, SwiGLU feed-forward, Rotary positional embeddings, and pre-norm RMSNorm. Long context is achieved with YARN+DCA at inference time, not at pre-training.

Note · the linked arXiv paper (2407.10671) is the Qwen2 technical report. Qwen2.5-7B reuses this same architecture verbatim, retraining on a larger 18T-token corpus.

02 · Layer pattern

The 28-layer stack, decoded

Unlike hybrid Mamba-Transformer designs, Qwen2-7B's stack is monotonous by design: every layer is the same decoder block — a GQA sublayer followed by a SwiGLU FFN sublayer, both wrapped in pre-norm RMSNorm and a residual add. The simplicity is the point — it makes the model trivially shardable and lets vendor kernels (FlashAttention, FlashInfer) plug in unchanged.

03 · The block

One decoder layer, in full

A single transformer block. The residual stream of width 3 584 flows top-to-bottom; at every sublayer, an RMSNorm copy is taken, transformed, and added back. This is the pre-norm formulation popularised by GPT-2 / Llama — both sublayers preserve the residual identity at initialization.

1 · Pre-attention RMSNorm
γ ∈ ℝ³⁵⁸⁴ · ε = 1e-6

Root-Mean-Square layer norm with a learned per-channel gain γ and no bias / no recentering. Cheaper than LayerNorm and numerically stable in bf16.

2 · GQA self-attention
28 Q · 4 KV · dh = 128

Three projections (q_proj, k_proj, v_proj) with QKV bias — a Qwen-specific carry-over from Qwen1; helpful for length extrapolation. Causal mask, FlashAttention-2 in practice. Output projection is bias-free.

3 · Residual add
x ← x + attn(norm(x))

Pre-norm placement: the residual rail is untouched and gradients flow cleanly. No DropPath, no LayerScale.

4 · Pre-FFN RMSNorm
second γ vector

A second, independent RMSNorm before the feed-forward sublayer.

5 · SwiGLU feed-forward
3 584 ↔ 18 944

Three linears (gate, up, down), zero bias. Gating is silu(gate(x)) ⊙ up(x), then projected back down. Replaces GeLU MLP from the original Transformer.

6 · Residual add
x ← x + ffn(norm(x))

Closes the block. The output enters the next layer (or, after layer 28, a final RMSNorm and the LM head).

04 · Primitives

Three operators carry the design

Every interesting choice in Qwen2-7B is at the operator level: how attention shares KV across heads, how the FFN gates its activations, how positional information enters. Below: the three primitives that distinguish this architecture from the original 2017 Transformer.

Sequence mixer · 28 of 28 layers

28 query heads share just 4 KV heads — a 7:1 ratio. The KV cache shrinks 7× vs MHA at near-identical quality. Q/K/V projections retain a learned bias term (Qwen-specific).

05 · GQA in detail

Why 28 query heads share 4 KV heads

The KV cache is the largest live tensor during autoregressive decoding — it grows linearly with context length and dominates HBM at long context. GQA decouples the count of Q heads (compute) from KV heads (memory), shrinking the cache 7× at this configuration while keeping the per-Q-head expressivity that pure MQA sacrifices.

28 Q → 4 KV grouping map

Heads 0–6 share KV-0. Heads 7–13 share KV-1. Heads 14–20 share KV-2. Heads 21–27 share KV-3. Each group is computed independently, then concatenated and projected.

06 · MoE sibling

Qwen2-57B-A14B — when the FFN goes sparse

Qwen2-7B itself is dense — every parameter fires for every token. The MoE in the Qwen2 family lives in a different family member, Qwen2-57B-A14B, where the SwiGLU FFN is replaced with a fine-grained Mixture-of-Experts. Same attention, same RoPE, same RMSNorm — only the FFN sublayer differs. Shown here for completeness.

64 routed + 8 shared experts

Every FFN sublayer is replaced with 64 tiny SwiGLU experts plus 8 always-on shared experts. A learned router picks the top-8 routed experts per token; the 8 shared experts always fire. Total params 57 B, only 14 B active per token.

07 · Tokenizer & pre-processing

Byte-level BPE, shared across the family

Every Qwen2 variant uses the same byte-level BPE vocabulary, inherited from Qwen1 and stable across the line. The byte-level fallback means any UTF-8 input is encodable; the 152 064-row table is sized to a multiple of 128 for matmul efficiency, even though only ~151 646 ids are populated.

Encode pipeline

A six-stage pipeline turns raw text into a tensor of token ids. The whole thing is reversible — bytes in, bytes out.

08 · Long context

From 4 K to 131 K, in stages

Qwen2-7B is not trained at 131 K. The pre-training context window grows in stages, and the final stretch to 131 K is a pure inference-time intervention — YARN re-scales RoPE frequencies and Dual Chunk Attention partitions the long sequence into local + cross chunks so the kernel never materializes a 131 K × 131 K attention map.

YARN

Rescales RoPE rotation frequencies non-uniformly: high-frequency dims (short-range) stay; low-frequency dims (long-range) are interpolated. Implemented as a multiplier on θⁱ. No re-training.

Dual Chunk Attention

Splits the sequence into chunks. Inside a chunk: standard causal attention. Across chunks: a re-scaled position index that keeps cross-chunk attention well-conditioned at distances beyond what RoPE has seen at training.

09 · Training pipeline

Pre-training, long-context, alignment

Qwen2-7B is trained in five visible stages — three of them pre-training, two of them post-training alignment. The bulk of the compute lives in stage 1; the rest is conditioning the model to be useful, honest, and coherent in long-form chat.

Pre-processing · pre-training

What goes in

  • Corpus — multilingual web, code, books, math; total 7 T tokens after dedup and quality filtering.
  • Languages — Chinese and English dominate; 27+ other languages including code-as-language.
  • Quality filtering — model-based scoring (using earlier Qwen checkpoints) to drop low-quality web pages.
  • Deduplication — minhash + exact-match dedup at document and substring level.
  • Packing — documents concatenated into 4 K (then 32 K) sequences with <|endoftext|> separators.
  • Tokenize — byte-level BPE, 152 064-row table, shared across the family.
Post-processing · alignment

What comes out

  • SFT — supervised fine-tuning on >500 K curated instruction-response pairs spanning chat, coding, math, agentic use.
  • Offline DPO — direct preference optimization against a static dataset of (chosen, rejected) pairs from humans and stronger models.
  • Online DPO — preference signal from a reward model trained on the same dataset; samples are drawn from the live policy and graded in the loop.
  • Chat format — ChatML-style with <|im_start|>role / <|im_end|> framing.
  • Safety — red-team prompts mixed into SFT and DPO; refusals are part of the preference signal.
  • Release — Base + Instruct weights, plus tokenizer and chat template, under Tongyi Qianwen license.
10 · The full family

How 7B fits among its siblings

Five released checkpoints, all sharing the same architecture skeleton — only widths, depths, and KV ratios change. The MoE member (57B-A14B) is the outlier: same shape outside the FFN, sparse and fine-grained inside it.
