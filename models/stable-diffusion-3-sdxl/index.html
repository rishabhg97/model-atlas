Stable Diffusion 3 and SDXL Architecture

Two generations of Stability AI's latent image generation models, side by side. SDXL keeps the U-Net diffusion backbone and adds a refiner; Stable Diffusion 3 replaces the U-Net entirely with a Multimodal Diffusion Transformer trained with rectified flow.

Architecture overview

A latent diffusion U-Net with a refiner (SDXL) versus a multimodal diffusion transformer trained with rectified flow (SD3). Both operate in latent space and share an encoder/decoder shape, but the denoising backbone is fundamentally different.

03

Block internals

Inside a single denoising block. SDXL fuses text into image features via cross-attention inside a U-Net block; SD3 carries two independent streams that meet in a joint attention operation.

SDXL · U-Net block with cross-attention

Spatial features pass through ResNet blocks and self-attention, then attend to the concatenated dual text embedding via cross-attention before being passed forward. Down-sampling and up-sampling halves of the U-Net are connected by skip connections.

SD3 · MMDiT block with joint attention

Two independent transformer streams — one for text tokens, one for image patch tokens — each have their own MLPs and norms. Inside the attention operation the two sequences are concatenated, so attention weights are computed jointly and information flows in both directions before each stream returns to its own MLP.

04

What changed from SDXL to SD3

Three architectural swaps account for most of the difference between the two systems.

01 · Denoiser

U-Net diffusion → Transformer rectified flow

U-Net · ε-prediction → MMDiT · rectified flow

SDXL keeps the convolutional U-Net with self-attention and cross-attention. SD3 replaces it with a transformer stack and switches the training objective to rectified flow, where data and noise are connected on a linear trajectory and a re-weighted schedule favors the middle of that trajectory.

02 · Quality lever

Refiner pass → no refiner

Base + refiner U-Nets → Single MMDiT

SDXL introduces a separate refinement model that runs post-hoc image-to-image on the base latent to improve fidelity. SD3 does not ship a refiner; the rectified-flow transformer produces final samples in one stack.

03 · Text conditioning

Dual text encoders → triple text embedders

2× CLIP → 2× CLIP + T5

SDXL widens cross-attention by concatenating two CLIP-family encoders. SD3 adds a T5 encoder to the two CLIPs and feeds the resulting text token sequence into a separate transformer stream that exchanges information with image tokens through joint attention.

Caveats and unknowns

Where the cited sources stop, this page stops. No values are inferred from community implementations or third-party reproductions.

Read these before quoting any number from this page

  • Variant parameter counts not reported SDXL's paper describes the U-Net as "three times larger" than prior Stable Diffusion, but does not give a single end-to-end parameter count for the cited variants. SD3 names a 800M-to-8B range and a 15-blocks/450M-to-38-blocks/8B scaling study, but per-variant breakdowns beyond those two endpoints are not stated in the cited research post. Verify each variant against its model card.
  • License verify model card SDXL code and weights are released via Stability-AI/generative-models. SD3's release terms are covered by Stability AI's licensing, which has multiple tiers; this page does not enumerate them. Treat license as variant- and date-specific.
  • Data mixture not reported Pretraining data composition, sourcing, and de-duplication procedures are not detailed in the cited research post; the SDXL paper abstract does not enumerate them either. Treat training-data claims as out of scope for this page.
  • Safety filters not reported Neither the SDXL paper abstract nor the SD3 research post enumerates the safety classifiers, watermarking, or content filters used at inference time. Refer to the model card and release notes for each specific variant.
  • Model-card restrictions verify model card Acceptable-use restrictions, commercial use terms, and downstream redistribution policies are variant-specific and may change between releases. Always consult the active model card.
  • No SDXL/SD3 detail mixing SDXL details on this page are sourced only from the SDXL paper and the linked code repository; SD3 details only from the SD3 paper and the Stability AI research post. U-Net/refiner facts are not extrapolated to SD3, and MMDiT/rectified-flow facts are not extrapolated to SDXL.
