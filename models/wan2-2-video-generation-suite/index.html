Wan2.2 Video Generation Suite + Wan2.1-VACE · FLF2V

An open, large-scale family of video diffusion models for text-to-video, image-to-video, first-and-last-frame to video, video editing, text-to-image, and audio/speech-driven video. Wan2.2 (Jul 28, 2025) introduces a two-expert Mixture-of-Experts diffusion design that splits denoising across timesteps, expanding total capacity to 27B parameters while keeping ~14B active per step[1]. Wan2.1-VACE (May 14, 2025) is the all-in-one editing variant whose unified architecture handles T2V, I2V, V2V, R2V, and masked V2V tasks from a single model[4][6]. FLF2V (Apr 17, 2025) conditions generation on a first and last frame[3].

Lineage: Wan2.1 → VACE / FLF2V → Wan2.2

Wan2.1 (technical report Mar 21, 2025) established the DiT + flow-matching stack with the 3D causal Wan-VAE, the umT5-xxl text encoder, and a multi-task scope covering T2V, I2V, video editing, text-to-image, and video-to-audio[7][8]. FLF2V (Apr 17, 2025) and Wan2.1-VACE (May 14, 2025) added first/last-frame conditioning and all-in-one editing on top of the Wan2.1 backbone[3][4][6]. Wan2.2 (Jul 28, 2025) replaces the dense 14B DiT with a two-expert MoE diffusion and introduces a higher-compression VAE[1][2]. Wan2.2-S2V (Aug 26, 2025) and Wan2.2-Animate (Sep 19, 2025) extend the suite to audio-driven and character-animation tasks[1].

!

What is not in this page

Per-checkpoint hidden dimensions, DiT depth/width, router thresholds beyond SNR_min / 2, exact frame counts per task, training-token totals, and independently-verified benchmark numbers are not reported in the listed public sources. See Unknowns & caveats.

§ 01 · End-to-end model map

Tokens, latents, denoising, frames

At inference the model maps a text prompt — optionally plus an image, first+last frames, a source video, masks, audio, or pose tracks — to a sequence of RGB video frames. Conditioning is encoded to features, the target video is denoised in latent space by the DiT backbone, and the Wan-VAE decoder lifts the denoised latent back to pixels.

§ 03 · Mixture-of-Experts diffusion

Two experts, one denoising trajectory

Wan2.2's A14B series splits the denoising trajectory across two specialised experts. A high-noise expert handles the early, layout-defining timesteps; a low-noise expert handles the later, detail-refining timesteps. Routing is timestep-driven by the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), not by a learned gate over tokens — so per-step compute is the same as a dense 14B DiT[1].

SNR-thresholded expert switch

Wan2.2 defines t_moe as the diffusion step at which SNR equals half of SNR_min (the minimum SNR observed across the trajectory, reached at maximum noise). For t > t_moe the high-noise expert is active. For t ≤ t_moe the low-noise expert is active[1].

tmoe · SNR = SNRmin / 2

Low-noise expert · detail

§ 04 · Latent encoder · Wan-VAE

3D causal VAE for variable-length video

The Wan family operates entirely in the latent space of a custom 3D causal VAE. Wan2.1's Wan-VAE encodes/decodes 1080P video of arbitrary length while preserving temporal information[7][8]. Wan2.2 ships an upgraded Wan2.2-VAE at higher compression to support the TI2V-5B 720P@24fps consumer-GPU path[1].

§ 05 · DiT backbone & objective

Flow Matching over latent video tokens

Wan is built on the mainstream Diffusion Transformer paradigm with the Flow Matching training objective[7][8]. The DiT consumes patched video latents plus conditioning tokens (text from umT5-xxl, plus task-specific image/audio adapters) and predicts the velocity field used to denoise the latent toward the target.

Backbone shape

  • Type: Diffusion Transformer (DiT)[7].
  • Objective: Flow matching[7].
  • Depth / hidden dim / heads: not reported in listed sources
  • Conditioning: cross-attention over text tokens (umT5-xxl) and task-adapter tokens (image / audio / pose / mask)[1].
  • Wan2.2 split: two-expert MoE at the diffusion-trajectory level (see §03).

Inference scaffold (from the repo)

  • Sharding: PyTorch FSDP for the DiT and the T5 (--dit_fsdp --t5_fsdp)[1].
  • Sequence parallel: DeepSpeed Ulysses (--ulysses_size 8)[1].
  • Attention: FlashAttention 3 on Hopper-class GPUs[1]; flash_attn dependency in requirements.txt[1].
  • Offload: --offload_model True, --convert_model_dtype, --t5_cpu for low-VRAM machines[1].
  • Wan-Animate default sampler: 20 inference steps, guidance scale 1.0[1].
§ 06 · Wan2.1-VACE control multiplexer

One model, five edit modes

VACE (Video-Aware Composable Editing) is the unified all-in-one editing variant on top of Wan2.1. The same network ingests text plus any combination of source video, spatial mask, and reference image(s), and exposes five editing capabilities through different combinations of those conditioning inputs[6][10]. VACE was accepted to ICCV 2025[10].

§ 11 · Unknowns & caveats

What is not in the listed sources

The following details would be needed for a fully reproducible architecture spec but are not published in the README, model cards, or VACE repository as of the snapshot used here.

Architecture

  • DiT layer count, hidden dim, attention heads, head dim for any checkpoint.
  • Wan-VAE latent channel count and decoder architecture details.
  • Exact form of the MoE router beyond the SNR-threshold rule.
  • Frame-count / temporal window per task at each resolution.
  • Whether the two MoE experts share early layers or are fully separate networks.
  • Cross-attention vs concatenation choice for image / audio / mask conditioning.
  • FLF2V conditioning mechanism (token concatenation, dual cross-attention, ...).

Training, data, benchmarks

  • Absolute training-set size (image and video counts, total frames, total tokens).
  • Compute budget, training duration, optimiser, learning-rate schedule.
  • Safety filtering / data-cleaning policy.
  • Per-task numerical benchmark scores (Wan-Bench 2.0 chart published as image only)[1].
  • FID / VBench / IS / motion-coherence / aesthetic-score values per checkpoint.
  • RLHF / DPO / step-distillation in the official release path (community distillations exist[1]).
!

Source-disagreement notes

Wan2.1-T2V-1.3B VRAM: 8.19 GB is cited by third-party guides (DigiAlps, PromptAA) attributed to the Wan2.1 README[3]. The official Wan2.1 README itself is not re-quoted here from a primary fetch in this snapshot, so the 8.19 GB figure should be re-verified against the upstream README before publishing it as primary.

Wan2.2-Animate diffusers note: the official README cautions that LoRAs trained on Wan2.2 are not recommended with Wan-Animate due to "weight changes during training"[1].
