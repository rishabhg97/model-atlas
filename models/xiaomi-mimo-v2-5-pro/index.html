Release · 2026-04-22 · Xiaomi MiMo

A 1.02 T MoE that runs at 42 B per token and sees 1 M tokens.

MiMo-V2.5-Pro is a Mixture-of-Experts decoder from Xiaomi: 1.02 trillion total parameters, 42 billion active per token, and a 1 M-token context window. Its signature is the architecture inherited from MiMo-V2-Flash: a hybrid attention stack that interleaves Sliding Window Attention (SWA) with Global Attention (GA) at a 6:1 ratio on a 128-token window, and a native 3-layer Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) head that triples decode throughput. Pre-training is FP8 mixed precision over 27 T tokens at a native 32 K sequence length; post-training is a three-stage SFT → Domain-Specialized RL → Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD) recipe targeting long-horizon agentic work.

01 · Forward pass

From text tokens through 70 hybrid-attention layers to four token predictions per step.

Text tokens enter a single embedding stream, traverse 70 decoder layers (one dense, sixty-nine MoE) with attention alternating between sliding-window and global at a fixed 6:1 cadence, then exit through the main LM head plus three lightweight MTP heads that predict tokens +2, +3, and +4 in parallel for speculative decoding.

02 · Layer stack

70 layers: a dense warm-up, then 69 MoE blocks under a 6:1 hybrid-attention cadence.

The model card reports 70 layers total — 1 dense + 69 MoE, with 10 Full (Global) Attention layers and 60 SWA layers. The published 6:1 SWA-to-GA ratio with 70 = 60 + 10 confirms the pattern: a 7-layer tile of SWA·SWA·SWA·SWA·SWA·SWA·GA repeats ten times across the depth.

Why this saves 7×. The Xiaomi release reports that interleaving SWA and GA at 6:1 "reduces KV-cache storage by nearly 7×" at long context while preserving performance, because only the 10 GA layers store full-context keys and values; the 60 SWA layers each cap their cache at 128 tokens. A learnable attention-sink bias compensates for the soft-max drift that SWA otherwise introduces.
03 · Decoder block

Pre-norm residual block — GQA on the attention arm, MoE on the feed-forward arm.

Every MiMo-V2.5-Pro layer is a standard pre-norm transformer block with two residual additions: one across attention, one across the feed-forward. The attention path is always GQA with 128 query heads and 8 KV heads; what changes between layers is the attention pattern (sliding-window or global) and the feed-forward arm (dense at layer 0, routed MoE everywhere else).

04 · Hybrid attention

Six sliding layers, one global, ten times — and a learnable sink bias.

Hybrid attention is MiMo-V2.5-Pro's defining choice. The official model card states the cadence directly: "Interleaves Sliding Window Attention (SWA) and Global Attention (GA) with a 6:1 ratio and 128 sliding window. This reduces KV-cache storage by nearly 7× while maintaining long-context performance via learnable attention sink bias."

05 · Attention shape

GQA · 128 query heads share 8 KV heads; QK and V have different head dimensions.

Inside every attention module (SWA or GA), the projection shape is the same: 128 query heads grouped onto 8 key/value heads (a 16:1 grouping ratio), with the unusual choice of asymmetric head dimensions — 192 for queries and keys, 128 for values. That asymmetry lets the QK score matrix be richer than the V projection that follows it.

Attention shape · ∀ ℓ

GQA · 128 / 8 · QK 192 · V 128

With 128 query heads sharing only 8 key/value heads, every group of 16 query heads reads the same KV pair. That cuts the KV cache by 16× before SWA even applies — and combines with the 6:1 hybrid pattern to deliver the overall ~7× cache reduction the model card cites.

The asymmetric head dimensions (d_qk = 192, d_v = 128) follow the DeepSeek-V family convention: queries and keys need extra width to keep the score matrix expressive, while values can stay narrower because they only ever get gathered, not compared.

06 · Feed-forward

384 routed experts · top-8 per token · 4.1 % of weights active.

On every MoE layer, the router scores 384 experts and forwards the token to its top 8. Each expert is a SwiGLU MLP with intermediate width 2,048. Activating 8 of 384 at one layer activates 8 / 384 ≈ 2.1 % of that layer's expert mass per token; aggregated across the whole model, the active parameter count lands at 42 B / 1.02 T = 4.1 %.

07 · Multi-Token Prediction

Three lightweight heads predict the next four tokens at once.

MiMo-V2.5-Pro is equipped with 3 MTP modules built from dense FFNs, natively integrated for both training and inference. The release describes them as tripling output speed during decode and accelerating RL rollouts. Unlike speculative decoding with a separate draft model, the MTP heads share the trunk: they are extensions of the same 70-layer stack, not an external speculator.

08 · Pre-training

27 trillion tokens, FP8 mixed precision, native 32 K — then extended to 1 M.

The release blog states pre-training runs on 27 T tokens at FP8 mixed precision with a native sequence length of 32 K; the 256 K context (base) and 1 M context (final) are reached by a context-extension phase that the post-write-up does not break down in detail.

09 · Post-training

SFT → Domain-Specialized RL → Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation.

Post-training follows the three-stage paradigm introduced in MiMo-V2-Flash. The decisive step is MOPD: one student model learns on-policy from its own rollouts under token-level guidance from multiple specialist teacher models, merging their capabilities into a single unified model rather than ensembling at inference time.

01 · SFT
Supervised Fine-Tuning

Foundational instruction-following on curated input/output pairs. Establishes the chat format, tool-call grammar, and reasoning channel.

curated pairs
02 · DST
Domain-Specialized Training

Separate teacher models are each optimized via domain-specific RL across math, safety, agentic tool-use, and more. The teachers are not deployed; they exist to teach the student.

multi-teacher RL
03 · MOPD
Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation

A single student model iteratively learns from its own outputs, receiving precise token-level guidance from every specialist teacher — unifying broad capabilities in one set of weights.

on-policy distillation

12 · Long context

GraphWalks at 1 M tokens — V2.5-Pro stays usable where V2-Pro collapses.

GraphWalks (OpenAI) fills the prompt with a directed graph of hex-hash nodes and asks the model to run a breadth-first search (BFS to depth N) or list a node's parents. The release blog evaluates the model across the full 32 K → 1 M input span. The headline result: past 128 K, MiMo-V2 Pro degrades rapidly and falls to 0.00 at 1 M on both subtasks, while MiMo-V2.5-Pro still scores meaningfully at half a million and beyond.

GraphWalks · score vs context length

Source-reported numbers from the MiMo-V2.5-Pro release blog. Two subtasks shown: BFS and Parents.
Mechanically. The 10 GA layers carry full-context signal end-to-end; the 60 SWA layers provide local refinement at a tiny cache cost; the learnable sink bias rescues the soft-max normalization that SWA distorts. Together they're what keeps the 1 M-token result above zero.
13 · Caveats & unknowns

What primary sources do not pin down — or pin down differently from the inventory.

Everything in this page is grounded in the official model card on Hugging Face (XiaomiMiMo/MiMo-V2.5-Pro) and the Xiaomi MiMo release blog. Where the inventory disagrees, the primary source wins. Where neither speaks, the field is marked unknown in source inventory.

Active parameter count: 42 B (not 43 B)

The inventory entry tags the model as 1.02T-A43B, but the official model card, release blog, and download table all state 42 B active parameters. This page uses the Xiaomi-reported figure. The page title preserves the inventory label so the entry remains identifiable.

Positional encoding · unknown in source inventory

The model card does not name a specific positional encoding (RoPE, ALiBi, NoPE, learned). Architecture is sliding-window + global, but the position scheme inside attention is not explicitly published in the released card.

Vocabulary size · unknown in source inventory

The Hugging Face safetensors index lists embedding tensor types but the model card does not publish the vocabulary size. Tokenizer family is not detailed beyond "BPE-style" by convention.

Shared MoE expert · not reported

The model summary publishes "Routed Experts: 384" and "Experts per Token: 8" but does not mention a shared expert. Some MoE designs in this family use one; without explicit confirmation, this page treats shared expert as not reported.

Router scoring & load balancing · not detailed

The model card does not detail the router function (sigmoid vs softmax top-k), auxiliary load-balancing loss, router-z loss, or expert-capacity factor. Specifics are deferred to the deployment cookbooks rather than the public model card.

Context extension · method not published

Pre-training is at a native 32 K sequence length and the final model serves 1 M tokens, but the extension method (YaRN, NTK, position-interpolation, continued training) is not detailed in the inventory or the public card.

Pre-training data mix · not published

The release names the budget (27 T tokens) but not the language mix, domain weighting, or curriculum schedule. Quality filters and deduplication are not described in the public release.

Benchmarks are source-reported

Every benchmark number on this page is taken directly from the Hugging Face model card, Xiaomi release blog, or the model's HF "Evaluation results" panel. None has been independently re-evaluated, and peer comparisons reflect each model's self-reported numbers, which were not produced under identical harnesses.
